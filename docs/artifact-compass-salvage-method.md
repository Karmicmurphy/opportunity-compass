# Artifact Compass - Deep Sea Salvage Method

This method defines how Opportunity Compass research should be done.

The goal is not to grab the obvious results sitting on top of search pages.

The goal is to dive wider, deeper, and become more selective until useful opportunity artifacts surface.

## Core idea

Most people skim the beach.

Opportunity Compass does salvage work.

It looks through messy platforms, boring posts, ignored tasks, confusing buyer requests, crowded listings, half-explained needs, and niche demand until it finds small opportunities that a regular person can understand, package, and judge safely.

## What counts as an artifact

An artifact is not just a job listing.

An artifact can be:

- a repeated buyer pain
- a strange service title
- a pricing pattern
- a warning pattern
- a tiny task hidden inside a big job post
- a beginner-safe slice of advanced work
- a delivery format people pay for
- a tool stack that keeps repeating
- a phrase buyers use when they do not know what they need
- a niche where AI helps but does not replace judgment

## What gets rejected

Reject shallow junk:

- generic prompt-engineer hype
- get-rich-quick claims
- vague remote work with no clear deliverable
- unpaid test traps
- requests for unnecessary account access
- expert-only work pretending to be entry level
- scraped repost garbage
- listings that say AI without showing real work
- offers that sound impressive but have no practical next step

## Pass structure

Each pass should get broader, wider, and pickier.

### Pass 1 - Surface Map

Purpose: Identify the obvious categories and source lanes.

Look at:

- Fiverr-style services
- Upwork-style buyer posts
- Indeed and ZipRecruiter-style job language
- AI trainer and evaluator platforms
- remote work safety sources

Output:

- source map
- first category list
- obvious warning signs
- first candidate cards

### Pass 2 - Pattern Salvage

Purpose: Find repeated patterns across different sources.

Look for:

- the same task under different names
- the same buyer pain across platforms
- the same warning sign with different wording
- small deliverables inside large jobs
- services that can be packaged for beginners

Output:

- stronger opportunity cards
- overlap map
- beginner-safe versus too-advanced split

### Pass 3 - Hidden Niche Dive

Purpose: Move beyond AI job boards and find local or small-business demand.

Look at:

- local business needs
- boring admin tasks
- review replies
- FAQ drafts
- listing cleanup
- PDF and spreadsheet cleanup
- Canva and presentation cleanup
- workflow documentation before automation

Output:

- Salvage Picks
- small paid offer ideas
- niche packaging rules

### Pass 4 - Trust and Risk Dive

Purpose: Build the bullshit filter.

Look at:

- official consumer guidance
- platform safety pages
- reporting about remote work risks
- common warning signs
- unsafe buyer behavior
- unclear payment or scope patterns

Output:

- risk-label rules
- red-flag checklist
- card scoring logic
- plain-English user warnings

### Pass 5 - Builder Readiness Dive

Purpose: Turn research into product structure.

Look for:

- fields the app needs
- filters users need
- card labels
- intake questions
- admin workflow
- pricing boundaries
- what should stay manual in v0.1

Output:

- final v0.1 card set
- database refinements
- screen copy
- builder prompt
- prototype requirements

## Optional extended passes

Use these only when needed.

### Pass 6 - Competitor Dive

Compare Opportunity Compass against job boards, AI job newsletters, side-gig blogs, freelance marketplaces, and safety-check tools.

### Pass 7 - Pricing Dive

Research what people pay for small AI-enabled services, curation products, newsletters, job boards, and niche paid communities.

### Pass 8 - SEO and Content Dive

Find search terms and content angles around AI side gigs, AI trainer jobs, prompt work, beginner freelance AI, and remote job safety.

### Pass 9 - Data Model Dive

Turn artifacts into structured fields, tags, categories, scoring logic, and admin review flows.

### Pass 10 - Prototype Handoff Dive

Prepare the full build packet for Z.ai, Codex, Lovable, or another builder.

## Research standards

Every pass should answer:

- What did we find?
- What is actually useful?
- What is shallow noise?
- What is too advanced?
- What is beginner-safe?
- What should become a card?
- What should become a warning?
- What should become a Salvage Pick?
- What should be added to the app spec?

## Escalation rules

### Use normal web research when:

- building source maps
- sampling current opportunities
- checking common patterns
- creating first cards
- updating product docs

### Use Deep Research when:

- a full competitive matrix is needed
- many sources need careful synthesis
- pricing needs to be benchmarked broadly
- a formal market report is needed
- trust and risk categories need official-source depth

### Use Agent when:

- building the prototype
- executing many repo changes
- creating branches and issues
- generating app code
- normalizing large datasets
- doing long autonomous implementation work

## Safety rule

Raw private material stays out of GitHub.

Only public-safe summaries, fake examples, original analysis, and safe product artifacts go into the public repo.
