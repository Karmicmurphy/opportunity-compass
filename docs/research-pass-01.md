## Strong findings

### 1. AI trainer/evaluator work is real, but unstable

AI training, data annotation, content review, response evaluation, red teaming, domain evaluation, and chatbot tutoring are real categories. They show up in remote contractor roles, job boards, and freelance marketplaces.

But they are not automatically beginner-safe. Risks include:

- inconsistent project availability
- sudden task changes
- unclear acceptance rules
- unpaid qualification tests
- account deactivation
- disturbing content exposure
- contractor status with no benefits
- opaque platform management

Opportunity Compass verdict tendency:

- Watch for general AI trainer roles
- Touch only when scope, pay, and task type are clear
- Too Advanced for expert-domain roles requiring credentials
- Scam Risk when pay sounds too good, process is vague, or fees are requested

### 2. AI content reviewer roles are a major category

Live samples showed roles like:

- AI content reviewer
- English writing reviewer
- language fluency AI trainer
- AI QA trainer
- image/video AI output reviewer
- finance AI evaluator
- clinical AI trainer
- domain-specific AI evaluator

These are useful for Opportunity Compass because they can be explained in plain English.

Beginner-safe versions usually involve:

- reading AI answers
- judging clarity and usefulness
- checking tone
- comparing two outputs
- writing feedback
- spotting errors

Riskier versions involve:

- medicine
- finance
- legal topics
- insurance/actuarial work
- red-team harmful content
- advanced coding
- expert credentials

### 3. Upwork contains strong opportunity examples beyond obvious AI jobs

Live samples showed not only AI trainer roles, but also practical small-business tasks:

- Canva presentation cleanup
- B2B LinkedIn content writing
- AI video editing for ads
- AI operations/project coordinator
- website maintenance and SEO
- creator partnerships support
- workflow/documentation work

These may be stronger for Salvage Picks than generic AI trainer jobs because they are tied to real buyer pain.

Opportunity Compass should translate these into beginner-adjacent cards like:

- Canva asset cleanup
- review reply batch
- FAQ draft
- product description cleanup
- workflow map before automation
- simple AI content review
- small-business prompt pack

### 4. Fiverr matters, but not as a connector

Fiverr is important as a market signal. Reporting shows Fiverr has pushed deeper into AI services through Fiverr Go and custom AI models for vetted freelancers.

But Fiverr public search access can be messy through general browsing, and there is no required Fiverr connector in this ChatGPT setup.

Use Fiverr as:

- evidence of service packaging
- pricing research
- trend research
- seller-side category research

Do not depend on Fiverr as infrastructure.

### 5. Prompt engineering should not be the product frame

Prompt engineer job postings appear rare compared with broader AI job categories. The useful skill pattern is not the title. It is:

- prompt design
- communication
- creative problem-solving
- AI tool fluency
- evaluation judgment
- domain explanation

Opportunity Compass should not sell users on becoming prompt engineers. It should help them package small AI-enabled services.

### 6. Scam filtering is central, not optional

AI job scams and task scams are increasing. Common patterns include:

- unsolicited recruiter messages
- too-good-to-be-true remote jobs
- vague duties
- urgent hiring
- requests for personal/financial information
- upfront fees
- fake equipment checks
- payment traps
- moving conversations off-platform too quickly
- asking the worker to pay to unlock tasks

Opportunity Compass should have scam-risk labels directly in every card.
