# Salvage Picks

Salvage Picks are overlooked, lower-competition, niche opportunities that beginners may not know how to find or package.

This section is not about reposting jobs. It is about interpreting hidden demand.

## Salvage Pick criteria

A good Salvage Pick usually has:

- a confused buyer
- a small annoying task
- low prestige but real value
- clear deliverable potential
- beginner-safe tools
- a way to package the service simply
- a clear owner approval step
- no need for account access
- no need for advanced engineering

## Bad Salvage Pick signs

Avoid niches that require:

- specialized professional advice
- account access
- misleading claims
- copied content
- advanced engineering
- unclear scope
- guaranteed outcomes

## Hidden Niche Dive findings

Small businesses are overloaded. The best entry points are not flashy AI jobs. They are boring, recurring, reviewable tasks where AI helps a human finish faster.

Strong hidden niches include:

- reviews and replies
- business profile cleanup notes
- FAQ drafts
- menu and service list cleanup
- PDF and spreadsheet cleanup
- social post batching
- Canva presentation cleanup
- estimate and invoice template cleanup
- customer message templates
- workflow maps before automation

## Strong Salvage Picks

### 1. Review Reply Packs for Local Businesses

Why overlooked: It does not sound like an AI job, but AI can help draft replies quickly.

Buyer need: A local business wants to respond to reviews without sounding robotic.

Package: 20 custom review replies in the business's tone.

Starter price: $25-$50.

Tools: ChatGPT, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, with common-sense review of tone and details.

Boundary: Draft replies only. The business owner approves and posts them.

### 2. Business Profile Cleanup Notes

Why overlooked: Many owners know their listing matters but do not know what is missing or outdated.

Buyer need: Their hours, services, description, photos, FAQs, or review replies need cleanup.

Package: One-page cleanup audit with suggested profile description, service list, FAQ, and review reply notes.

Starter price: $35-$100.

Tools: Browser, ChatGPT, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if this is an audit and copy draft only.

Boundary: No login needed. Deliver notes and drafts for owner review.

### 3. FAQ Drafts for Small Service Businesses

Why overlooked: It is basic, but many small businesses do not have clear website answers.

Buyer need: Reduce repeated customer questions.

Package: 10-20 FAQ questions and answers.

Starter price: $35-$100.

Tools: ChatGPT, business notes, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, as long as the owner reviews the final answers.

Boundary: Owner approves final answers before publishing.

### 4. Menu or Service List Cleanup

Why overlooked: Menus, price sheets, and service lists are often messy, old, or hard to read.

Buyer need: Make services, prices, options, and descriptions clearer for customers.

Package: Cleaned service list or menu copy in a simple document.

Starter price: $25-$100.

Tools: ChatGPT, Google Docs, spreadsheet app, optional Canva.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if the buyer supplies exact facts and approves final copy.

Boundary: Do not invent prices, ingredients, terms, or guarantees.

### 5. Marketplace Listing Cleanup

Why overlooked: It looks like simple writing work, but sellers often need it badly.

Buyer need: Rough listings that do not explain products clearly.

Package: Clean up 10 product descriptions and titles.

Starter price: $35-$75.

Tools: ChatGPT, spreadsheet or document editor.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if all product facts come from the seller.

Boundary: Improve clarity without adding unsupported claims.

### 6. Old PDF to Clean Spreadsheet

Why overlooked: It looks boring and annoying, so many people skip it.

Buyer need: Data trapped in old PDFs, menus, lists, schedules, or forms.

Package: Convert one small PDF into a clean spreadsheet.

Starter price: $25-$100 depending on size.

Tools: PDF reader, spreadsheet app, ChatGPT for cleanup support.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if the data is not sensitive and the scope is small.

Boundary: Confirm file size, columns, and sensitivity before starting.

### 7. Social Post Batch from Existing Business Info

Why overlooked: Owners need posts, but they do not want a full marketing agency.

Buyer need: Turn existing services, photos, seasonal offers, and FAQs into a small batch of posts.

Package: 10 simple Facebook or Instagram posts with captions and image ideas.

Starter price: $35-$100.

Tools: ChatGPT, Canva, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if using owner-provided facts and images.

Boundary: Use real business facts only.

### 8. Canva Flyer or One-Page Handout Cleanup

Why overlooked: Many businesses already have rough flyers. They need cleanup, not a full design agency.

Buyer need: Make an existing flyer readable, aligned, and professional.

Package: Clean up one flyer or one-page handout in Canva.

Starter price: $25-$75.

Tools: Canva, ChatGPT for copy tightening.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if content and branding are provided.

Boundary: Use buyer-provided assets or clearly licensed assets.

### 9. Estimate, Invoice, or Message Template Cleanup

Why overlooked: This is boring operational writing, but it saves real time.

Buyer need: Better templates for estimates, invoices, appointment reminders, follow-ups, and customer messages.

Package: 5-10 cleaned message templates.

Starter price: $35-$100.

Tools: ChatGPT, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, for general service communication.

Boundary: Keep templates practical and have the business approve them.

### 10. Workflow Written Down Before Automation

Why overlooked: Everyone talks about automation, but many buyers cannot explain their process.

Buyer need: Turn a messy repeated task into a clear workflow before any automation is built.

Package: One-page workflow map plus recommended tools.

Starter price: $35-$150.

Tools: ChatGPT, Google Docs, simple flowchart or mapping tool.

Beginner-safe: Medium. Good for people who can ask clear questions and document steps.

Boundary: This is planning, not a promise to build a full automation.

### 11. Customer Question Inbox Sorting

Why overlooked: It sounds too simple, but many small businesses have repeated messages scattered across text, email, social platforms, and forms.

Buyer need: Group repeated customer questions into categories and draft reusable replies.

Package: Sort 50 common messages into themes and draft 10 reusable replies.

Starter price: $50-$150.

Tools: ChatGPT, spreadsheet, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Medium, if messages are cleaned and shared safely.

Boundary: Work from sanitized examples whenever possible.

### 12. Before-and-After Website Copy Cleanup

Why overlooked: Many small business websites are outdated but do not need a full rebuild.

Buyer need: Make homepage or service copy clearer without changing the site directly.

Package: One-page copy cleanup: current text, improved text, and notes.

Starter price: $50-$150.

Tools: Browser, ChatGPT, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, for general service businesses.

Boundary: Improve clarity without promising search ranking results.

## Best first paid offers

Start with small, fixed-scope offers:

- 20 review replies for $25-$50
- 10 FAQ answers for $35-$75
- 10 product descriptions for $35-$75
- 1 PDF to spreadsheet for $25-$100
- 10 social posts for $35-$100
- 1 Canva flyer cleanup for $25-$75
- 5 message templates for $35-$100
- 1 workflow map for $50-$150

## Product implication

Opportunity Compass should treat Salvage Picks as packages, not listings.

Each Salvage Pick should show:

- buyer pain
- smallest paid offer
- tools needed
- deliverable checklist
- boundary
- beginner fit
- where to find buyers
- what not to promise
