# Salvage Picks

Salvage Picks are overlooked, lower-competition, niche opportunities that beginners may not know how to find or package.

This section is not about reposting jobs. It is about interpreting hidden demand.

## Salvage Pick criteria

A good Salvage Pick usually has:

- a confused buyer,
- a small annoying task,
- low prestige but real value,
- clear deliverable potential,
- beginner-safe tools,
- and a way to package the service simply.

## Bad Salvage Pick signs

Avoid niches that require:

- regulated legal/medical/financial advice,
- account access,
- deception,
- fake reviews,
- spam,
- copyright imitation,
- advanced engineering,
- or risky platform behavior.

## Example Salvage Picks

### 1. Review Reply Packs for Local Businesses

Why overlooked: It does not sound like an AI job, but AI can help draft replies quickly.

Buyer need: A local business wants to respond to reviews without sounding robotic.

Package: 20 custom review replies in the business's tone.

Starter price: $25-$50.

Tools: ChatGPT, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, with common-sense review of tone and details.

### 2. Old PDF to Clean Spreadsheet

Why overlooked: It looks boring and annoying, so many people skip it.

Buyer need: Data trapped in old PDFs, menus, lists, schedules, or forms.

Package: Convert one small PDF into a clean spreadsheet.

Starter price: $25-$100 depending on size.

Tools: PDF reader, spreadsheet app, ChatGPT for cleanup support.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if the data is not sensitive and the scope is small.

### 3. FAQ Drafts for Small Service Businesses

Why overlooked: It is basic, but many small businesses do not have clear website answers.

Buyer need: Reduce repeated customer questions.

Package: 10-20 FAQ questions and answers.

Starter price: $35-$100.

Tools: ChatGPT, business notes, Google Docs.

Beginner-safe: Yes, as long as regulated advice is avoided or reviewed by the business.

### 4. Marketplace Listing Cleanup

Why overlooked: It looks like simple writing work, but sellers often need it badly.

Buyer need: Rough listings that do not explain products clearly.

Package: Clean up 10 product descriptions and titles.

Starter price: $35-$75.

Tools: ChatGPT, spreadsheet or document editor.

Beginner-safe: Yes, if no false claims are added.

### 5. Workflow Written Down Before Automation

Why overlooked: Everyone talks about automation, but many buyers cannot explain their process.

Buyer need: Turn a messy repeated task into a clear workflow before any automation is built.

Package: One-page workflow map plus recommended tools.

Starter price: $35-$100.

Tools: ChatGPT, Google Docs, simple flowchart/mapping tool.

Beginner-safe: Medium. Good for people who can ask clear questions and document steps.
