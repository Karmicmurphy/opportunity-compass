# Research Pass 06 - Competitor and Pricing Dive

Date: 2026-07-02

## Purpose

This pass checks where Opportunity Compass fits against existing job boards, freelance marketplaces, AI task platforms, newsletters, and remote-work products.

## Core finding

Opportunity Compass should not compete as a larger job board.

It should compete as a translation layer and judgment layer.

Most platforms answer:

- What jobs exist?
- Where can I apply?
- What company is hiring?

Opportunity Compass answers:

- What does this actually mean?
- Can a beginner touch it?
- What is the smallest safe paid offer?
- What should I ask before acting?
- What is the risk?
- What should I avoid?

## Competitor categories

### 1. General job boards

Examples:

- Indeed
- LinkedIn Jobs
- ZipRecruiter
- Glassdoor
- Monster

Strengths:

- huge inventory
- employer reach
- alerts
- search filters

Weaknesses:

- noisy
- scam exposure
- weak beginner translation
- not focused on small AI-enabled work
- entry-level often not truly entry-level

Opportunity Compass position:

Do not try to beat them on inventory. Use them as source signals.

### 2. Remote/flexible job boards

Examples:

- FlexJobs
- Remote.co
- We Work Remotely
- Working Nomads

Strengths:

- remote-first filtering
- some screening
- better trust than raw boards

Weaknesses:

- still job-listing oriented
- not focused on AI side gigs
- not built around tiny paid offers

Opportunity Compass position:

Focus on opportunity translation, not pure remote search.

### 3. Freelance marketplaces

Examples:

- Fiverr
- Upwork
- Freelancer.com
- PeoplePerHour
- Guru
- Contra

Strengths:

- buyer demand
- seller packaging examples
- real budgets
- service categories

Weaknesses:

- crowded
- race-to-bottom pricing
- platform friction
- beginners struggle to interpret what buyers really need

Opportunity Compass position:

Use these as pattern mines, then translate gigs into safer starter packages.

### 4. AI task and data platforms

Examples:

- Outlier
- Remotasks
- DataAnnotation
- Appen
- TELUS Digital
- OneForma
- Mercor
- Prolific
- Alignerr/OpenTrain-style platforms

Strengths:

- real AI-adjacent work
- flexible contractor tasks
- growing demand for evaluation and labeling

Weaknesses:

- inconsistent availability
- opaque task approval
- qualification friction
- domain expertise traps
- platform dependency

Opportunity Compass position:

Explain task types, risks, and beginner fit. Do not pretend they are guaranteed income.

### 5. AI job newsletters and AI career sites

Examples:

- AI job newsletters
- niche AI job boards
- AI career lists
- prompt engineering role roundups

Strengths:

- curated AI roles
- niche audience
- trend awareness

Weaknesses:

- often career-focused, not side-gig focused
- often role titles over practical task translation
- may overemphasize prompt engineering

Opportunity Compass position:

Focus on plain-English cards and small paid offers.

## Pricing signal

A useful public benchmark is FlexJobs. A 2026 job-site review reported FlexJobs pricing at about $9.95 for one week or $59.95 per year for access to remote/flexible job listings and features.

Opportunity Compass should start lower and simpler because it is not a full job board yet.

## Suggested pricing for v0.1 mockup

### Free

- limited opportunity feed
- basic verdict
- basic beginner fit
- basic risk label

### Paid

- Salvage Picks
- Daily Top 5
- saved opportunities
- suggested first messages
- deliverable templates
- niche breakdowns
- risk checklist

### Suggested starter prices

- $5/month
- $29/year

Reason:

This keeps the product accessible while testing whether people value the translation layer.

## Strong positioning line

Opportunity Compass is not another place to scroll jobs.

It is a plain-English opportunity translator and bullshit filter for small AI-enabled work.

## What not to build

Do not build a huge searchable job board first.
Do not charge like an enterprise tool.
Do not promise income.
Do not hide the risk labels behind a paywall.
Do not compete on volume.

## What to build

- curated cards
- beginner fit labels
- risk labels
- Salvage Picks
- first-message templates
- deliverable checklists
- small paid offer packaging
- manual admin review

## Product moat

The moat is not data volume.

The moat is judgment:

- plain-English translation
- risk filtering
- beginner fit
- practical packaging
- overlooked niche discovery
- small deliverable clarity
