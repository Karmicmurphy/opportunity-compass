# Sample Opportunities

These are public-safe, original sample cards for Opportunity Compass. They are based on repeated market patterns, not copied private listings. Do not replace them with private emails, private screenshots, real user data, or copied paid-platform content.

## 1. AI Content Reviewer - English Writing

- Source: Pattern salvage / public-safe sample
- Category: AI content review
- Beginner Fit: Medium
- Verdict: Watch
- Estimated Pay: $10-$40/hour depending on platform and skill fit
- Estimated Time: Flexible contractor blocks
- Tools Needed: ChatGPT, grammar checker, document editor, platform rubric
- Skills Needed: Clear writing, careful reading, tone judgment, instruction following
- What This Really Means: Read AI-generated writing and judge whether it is clear, accurate, useful, and natural.
- Hidden Buyer Pain: AI companies need humans to catch weak writing, vague answers, hallucinations, and tone problems.
- Why Beginners Miss It: It sounds technical, but the beginner-safe slice is mostly reading, comparing, and explaining what is wrong.
- Smallest Paid Offer: Review 10 AI answers against a simple checklist for a fixed test batch.
- Suggested First Message: I can help review AI-written content for clarity, tone, and usefulness. Send a sample rubric or 3 examples of good versus bad answers and I will match that standard.
- Questions to Ask: What rubric do you use? How long is each answer? Is this general writing or expert-domain content? How is acceptance judged?
- Deliverable Checklist: Reviewed outputs, pass/fail notes, short explanation, flagged issues, final spreadsheet or platform submission.
- Red Flags: Unpaid long test, unclear acceptance rules, sensitive expert topics without credentials, vague pay, no dispute process.
- Green Flags: Clear rubric, paid trial, general content, defined output count, transparent pay.
- Salvage Pick: No

## 12. Remote AI Trainer Scam-Risk Check

- Source: Pattern salvage / public-safe sample
- Category: Trust and risk filter
- Beginner Fit: High
- Verdict: Scam Risk if warning signs appear
- Estimated Pay: Not a job card; this is a safety-check card
- Estimated Time: 5-15 minutes per listing
- Tools Needed: Browser, checklist, company website search, platform verification
- Skills Needed: Skepticism, verification, reading details carefully
- What This Really Means: Before touching a remote AI trainer listing, check whether the offer has basic signs of a real job or a risky trap.
- Hidden Buyer Pain: Beginners are targeted by vague remote work because they want flexible income and may overlook warning signs.
- Why Beginners Miss It: The listing uses AI language, flexible hours, and high pay to sound legitimate.
- Smallest Paid Offer: Not applicable. This card prevents bad action.
- Suggested First Message: Before I apply, I need the official company page, task description, payment method, and whether any unpaid test or fee is required.
- Questions to Ask: Is this posted on the official company site? Is there any upfront payment? Is the recruiter using a real company email? What exactly is the task?
- Deliverable Checklist: Company verified, no upfront fee, payment terms clear, task clear, platform legitimate, personal data protected.
- Red Flags: Upfront fee, private messaging pressure, vague tasks, unrealistic pay, fake check, request for banking info, no company verification.
- Green Flags: Official company domain, clear role, no fees, written contract, platform-protected payment.
- Salvage Pick: No
