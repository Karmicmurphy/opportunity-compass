# Source Map

This document tracks the public research lanes for Opportunity Compass.

## Purpose

Opportunity Compass needs a wide source map so it does not become one narrow job-board clone.

The goal is to inspect several kinds of opportunity sources, then translate patterns into beginner-safe opportunity cards.

## Source categories

### Freelance marketplaces

Use for seller-side and buyer-side research.

Sources to check:

- Fiverr
- Upwork
- Freelancer.com
- PeoplePerHour
- Guru
- Contra
- Toptal
- Codeable
- 99designs
- Dribbble
- Behance
- Botpool or other AI-focused freelance marketplaces

What to extract:

- common service titles
- common pricing ranges
- beginner-safe service patterns
- overcrowded categories
- overhyped claims
- niche services
- red flags

### Job boards

Use for mainstream job-language research.

Sources to check:

- Indeed
- ZipRecruiter
- LinkedIn Jobs
- FlexJobs
- Remote.co
- We Work Remotely
- Wellfound
- Otta / Welcome to the Jungle
- Built In
- Dice

What to extract:

- AI evaluator roles
- AI trainer roles
- prompt evaluator roles
- content quality roles
- data annotation roles
- AI support roles
- AI operations assistant roles
- entry-level traps
- fake-entry-level requirements

### AI task and data platforms

Use for AI-adjacent microtask and evaluation work.

Sources to check:

- Outlier
- Remotasks
- DataAnnotation
- Appen
- TELUS International AI
- RWS / TrainAI
- OneForma
- Invisible Technologies
- Surge AI
- Scale AI ecosystem

What to extract:

- real task categories
- qualification requirements
- pay transparency or lack of it
- platform risk
- account deactivation risk
- inconsistent work supply
- beginner fit

### Developer and builder gig sources

Use for higher-skill opportunities and boundary-setting.

Sources to check:

- GitHub issue bounties
- Gitcoin
- Bountysource-style platforms if active
- Replit community opportunities
- Indie Hackers requests
- Hacker News Who is Hiring / Freelancer threads
- Codeable
- Toptal
- Upwork developer projects

What to extract:

- what is too advanced
- what could be beginner-safe if reframed
- small documentation tasks
- QA/testing tasks
- prompt/testing tasks
- no-code/light-code tasks

### Local and small-business sources

Use for overlooked Salvage Picks.

Sources to check:

- Craigslist
- Facebook groups
- Nextdoor
- local business websites
- Google Business Profiles
- chamber of commerce directories
- local service ads

What to extract:

- review reply needs
- menu/listing cleanup
- FAQ drafts
- website copy cleanup
- PDF-to-spreadsheet tasks
- social post batching
- basic AI setup help

### Scam and safety sources

Use for red-flag rules.

Sources to check:

- FTC
- FBI / IC3
- consumer protection agencies
- major reporting on job scams
- platform safety pages
- academic scam research

What to extract:

- upfront fee warnings
- task scam patterns
- fake check/equipment scam patterns
- crypto/payment traps
- identity theft risks
- account-access risks
- platform rule violations

## Current research note

A first broad public-source pass found useful material around:

- AI/data annotation work
- task scams
- remote job scams
- Fiverr AI service trends
- Upwork and freelance marketplace changes
- prompt engineering rarity and skill profile
- small-business AI adoption friction

Fiverr-specific public search is not always cleanly accessible through general web search, so screenshots or manual exports may still be useful later. That does not make Fiverr a required connector.
